## Not your average civilian
While you read through this primer, keep in mind that the following descriptions are for the general cases or average experiences for the people of Melako Valley. You, as a Player Character, are not expected to be average; if you have any ideas that break the mold but still feel setting appropriate, go for it! If your idea feels pretty out there, run it by me (Phil) first. Most likely we'll work together to integrate it into the world and you'll have your own piece of the setting to call your own. Worst case I'll just say no and you'll have to come up with something else.

## Culture of the valley
The harsh climate of Melako Valley nurtures a dog eat dog, every man for himself mentality amongst the people of the valley. Nothing comes for free and if something sounds too good to be true, it probably is. The one exception is external threats to a town; feuding neighbors become blood brothers when their home is under threat. External threats usually come from creatures emerging from underground or raiders attacking to poach resources on the surface.

The valley itself is extremely isolated; as far as anyone knows, the people of Melako Valley are the only people in the world. They are surrounded by a brutal desert that none have ever traversed. 

## Technology level
The level of technology that the current population can produce is early American wild west with one big asterisk: industry is choked by the small population and scarcity of water. Electricity has been discovered and can be created via steam generators, but water must be carefully rationed and there is no electrical grid system. Inventors and mechanics might have a generator in their workshop to power machinery, but they are the exception rather than the rule. Most buildings are still burning a type of oil harvested by beetle farmers for light and warmth.

Similarly, guns exist but most people consider them less reliable than traditional ranged weaponry because gun and ammunition manufacturing is so time-consuming and difficult. Many gun wielders choose to do their own repairs and ammo manufacturing rather than trust their livelihood to a mechanic. Rather than deal with that, most people opt for what can be more easily crafted by modern conventions: roughly steel age weapons and armor.

More advanced technology does exist as artifacts left behind by the Ancient Ones, but the methods of their creation is a mystery to the valley's inhabitants. There are only a few mechanics and inventors whom are trusted to work with these artifacts without breaking them. All three of the major towns within Melako Valley are built around ancient ones' Massive Aqua Condensors that create water from thin air. This is the only ancient tech with which most people will ever interact. Many Delvers are motivated by the chance to find powerful ancient artifacts that lie undiscovered deep underground.

## Daily life in Vaanchor
The campaign starts in Vaanchor, the westernmost town in Melako Valley with a population of roughly 1000. Built in the shade of a massive cylindrical tower left behind by the ancients, Vaanchor was the last of the three major towns settled in the valley. Like the other two towns, Vaanchor's MAC provides water to the townsfolk under the watchful eye of the local quartermaster. All the trades of modern Melako Valley life can be found here: bakers, barbers, butchers, brewers, doctors, masons, tailors, and more. Gold, silver, and copper are exchanged between people for goods and services across the valley at a rate of 1gp : 10sp : 100cp. The town is walled by stone and scraps of ancient metal to keep out predators and raiders. Outside the walls is a large entrance to the underground, both it and the walls monitored by town guards who watch for threats to Vaanchor.

Cave Runners use the uppermost levels of the extensive cave system beneath the valley to move goods and information between the surface settlements, for a price. Delvers push deeper into the caves to discover lost artifacts or harvest resources necessary for tradesmen on the surface to craft their wares.
